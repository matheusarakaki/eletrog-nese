1880s • 1888

Telautograph

The Telautograph was one of the first facsimile devices, allowing the transmission of handwriting over a distance via electrical signals, a precursor to modern communication technologies. Developed by Elisha Gray, the system worked through two electrically connected pens - when a person wrote with a transmitting pen, the receiving pen reproduced exactly the same movements on distant paper. This technology was widely used in commercial and military applications, establishing the foundations for the later development of fax and other image transmission technologies.

Inventor: Elisha Gray (1835-1901), American inventor

Historical Context

Telautograph emerged amid a period of great conflict, one of which was the War of the Pacific, which was caused by territorial disputes between Chile and the alliance between Bolivia and Peru.
