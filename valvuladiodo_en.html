John Fleming's Diode-Valve

John Ambrose Fleming invented the thermionic valve (diode), the first non-solid-state electronic device, which would become fundamental to the development of modern electronics. Fleming's valve worked by allowing electrons to flow in only one direction between two electrodes in a heated vacuum tube. This revolutionary discovery established the fundamental principles of electronics and made possible the development of amplifiers, radio receivers, and eventually, electronic computers.

Inventor: John Ambrose Fleming (1849-1945), English electrical engineer

Historical Context

At the turn of the 20th century, wireless telegraphy was in its early stages, but radio receivers of the time used inefficient detectors, such as the crystal detector, to convert radio waves (alternating current) into a readable direct current signal.
