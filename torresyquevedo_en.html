1910s • 1913

Torres y Quevedo's Analytical Machine

Torres y Quevedo developed an electromechanical analytical machine that represented a significant advance over purely mechanical designs, introducing electrical elements into computation. His machine used electromechanical relays to control operations, representing one of the first practical implementations of electrical control logic in computing devices. This work established fundamental principles that would later be used in the development of the first electronic computers.

Inventor: Leonardo Torres y Quevedo (1852-1936), Spanish engineer and mathematician

Historical Context

The historical context of Leonardo Torres y Quevedo's activity in the early 20th century, especially between 1910 and 1913, falls within a period of intense technological innovation and exploration of the possibilities of automation and mechanical and electromechanical logic.
