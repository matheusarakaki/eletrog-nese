1910s • 1911

CTR: Computing-Tabulating-Recording Company

The formation of CTR through the merger of various technology companies created one of the first corporations focused on data processing equipment, a direct precursor to IBM. The company combined Herman Hollerith's tabulating technologies, employee time recording systems, and commercial weighing machines. This strategic merger demonstrated the emerging commercial potential of information processing technology and established a model for future technology corporations.

Founder: Charles Ranlett Flint (1859-1934), American businessman

Historical Context

The Computing-Tabulating-Recording Company (CTR) is directly linked to the emergence of IBM (International Business Machines) in the early 20th century and the need for automated calculations and data management in a pre-electronic era. CTR was a holding company that consolidated several companies in 1911, each with its own specialty, to create a powerhouse in the field of data processing equipment.
