1880s
1886

Burroughs

William Seward Burroughs developed the first commercially successful mechanical calculator.

1888

Telautograph

One of the first facsimile devices, allowing the transmission of handwriting over distance.

1890s
1893

Analog Calculators

Leonardo Torres y Quevedo developed analog calculators for complex computations.

1897

The CRT Monitor

Karl Ferdinand Braun invented the cathode-ray tube (CRT), the basis of monitors and TVs.

1898

Telegraphone

Valdemar Poulsen invented the Telegraphone, the first practical magnetic recorder.

1900s
1903

Percy Ludgate's Analytical Engine

Percy Ludgate designed a more compact and efficient analytical engine.

1904

Liquid Crystal (LCD)

Friedrich Reinitzer discovered liquid crystals, the basis for future LCD screens.

1904

John Fleming's Diode Valve

John Fleming created the first solid-state electronic device (diode).

1910s
1911

CTR: Computing-Tabulating-Recording Company

The merger of technology companies gave rise to CTR, the predecessor of IBM.

1913

Torres y Quevedo's Analytical Engine

Torres y Quevedo developed an advanced electromechanical analytical engine.

1918

Flip-Flop

William Eccles and F.W. Jordan invented the flip-flop circuit, the basis of digital electronics.

1920s
1924

IBM

CTR was renamed International Business Machines (IBM), marking the beginning of an era.
